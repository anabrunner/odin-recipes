Porkolt

Image by: Orrling

Description

Porkolt is a Hungarian stew dish made primarily with onions, boneless meat, and paprika. The recipe is very simple and only requires a few ingredients. Due to the simplicity, it is very important to use quality ingredients, especially the paprika.

The following is a family recipe from my partner's Hungarian grandmother.

Ingredients

  • 1 large onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 2 tbsp canola or vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 2 tbsp good quality paprika
  • 0.5kg meat (any meat of your choice is fine: chicken, pork, beef, etc)
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 small can (400mL) of diced or whole tomato
  • 1 tsp caraway seed (optional if using pork)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Sour cream for serving

Steps

  1. Finely chop the onion and garlic and place in a large pot or dutch oven.
  2. Add the oil and salt and cook on low until the onions are soft.
  3. Remove the pot from heat and wait 5 minutes before adding the paprika. This is important otherwise the paprika will burn!
  4. Mix the paprika well and return the pot to low heat.
  5. Dice the meat and add to the pot. Add the canned tomato and tomato paste. Do not drain the tomato can before adding.
  6. If using pork as your meat, add the caraway seed.
  7. Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Cover pot and let simmer on low heat for about an hour.
  8. Serve over egg noodles with sour cream.
